I’ve been reading. The examples are slightly dated, yet the they are fun and relevant examples to discuss the authors main points, and are as relevant today

what the main questions are to ask yourself to avoid the pitfalls discussed in the book:

  1. What biases are introduced by the sampling procedure? What exact population am I sampling from?
  2. What has not been reported? Multiple testing?
  3. How large is the variance?
  4. Are two different numbers actually statistically and practially significantly different?
  5. In what way might the graph distort the representation of the effect?
  6. Does the size depicted correspond to the area or to a single dimension in the picture?
  7. Is what I measure directly related to what I want to measure?

It is sometimes a substantial service simply to point out that a subject in controversy is not as open-and-shut as it has been made to seem.
